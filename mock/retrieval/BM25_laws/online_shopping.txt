1. Directive 2011/83/EU (Consumer Rights Directive): Governs distance contracts, information requirements, delivery obligations, right of withdrawal, and remedies for non-delivery.
2. Directive 2005/29/EC (Unfair Commercial Practices Directive): Prohibits misleading and aggressive commercial practices by traders toward consumers.
3. Directive 93/13/EEC (Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Directive): Protects consumers from unfair terms in standard contracts.
4. Directive 1999/44/EC (Sale of Consumer Goods and Associated Guarantees): Sets rules on conformity of goods, remedies for non-conformity, and seller liability.
5. Directive 2000/31/EC (Electronic Commerce Directive): Establishes legal framework for online services, including transparency and contract requirements.
6. Regulation (EU) 2017/2394 (Consumer Protection Cooperation Regulation): Enhances cross-border enforcement of consumer protection laws and cooperation between national authorities.
7. Regulation (EU) No 524/2013 (Online Dispute Resolution Regulation): Establishes the ODR platform for resolving consumer-trader disputes arising from online sales.
8. Directive (EU) 2019/2161 (Modernisation Directive): Updates and strengthens consumer protection rules, especially for digital and cross-border transactions.
9. Directive 2007/64/EC (Payment Services Directive): Provides legal basis for chargebacks and consumer rights in payment transactions.
10. Directive 2008/48/EC (Consumer Credit Directive): Covers credit-related aspects of consumer purchases and payment disputes.
11. Directive 97/7/EC (Distance Selling Directive): Predecessor to the Consumer Rights Directive, relevant for historical cases.
12. Directive 2002/65/EC (Distance Marketing of Financial Services Directive): Applies to financial services sold at a distance.
13. Directive 2019/770/EU (Digital Content and Services Directive): Sets rules for contracts involving digital content and services.
14. Directive 2019/771/EU (Sale of Goods Directive): Updates consumer rights for the sale of goods, including remedies for non-delivery.
15. Directive 98/6/EC (Price Indication Directive): Ensures clear price information for consumers.
16. Directive 2002/58/EC (ePrivacy Directive): Regulates privacy and electronic communications in e-commerce.
17. Regulation (EU) 2015/848 (Insolvency Regulation): Addresses consumer claims in trader insolvency.
18. Directive 2013/11/EU (Alternative Dispute Resolution Directive): Provides for ADR mechanisms for consumer disputes.
19. Regulation (EC) No 861/2007 (European Small Claims Procedure): Enables consumers to pursue small cross-border claims efficiently.
20. Directive 2008/52/EC (Mediation Directive): Facilitates mediation in civil and commercial matters.
21. Directive 2009/22/EC (Injunctions Directive): Allows consumer organizations to seek injunctions against traders infringing consumer rights.
22. Directive 2014/104/EU (Antitrust Damages Directive): Relevant for collective redress actions.
23. Regulation (EU) 2018/302 (Geo-blocking Regulation): Prohibits unjustified geo-blocking and discrimination based on nationality or residence.
24. Article 169 of the Treaty on the Functioning of the European Union (TFEU): Provides the legal basis for EU consumer protection policy.
25. General Product Safety Directive (2001/95/EC): Ensures that products placed on the market are safe for consumers.
